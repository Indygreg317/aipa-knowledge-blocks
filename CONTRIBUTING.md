# Contributing to AIPA Knowledge Blocks

Thank you for your interest in contributing.

AIPA is an open framework designed to evolve through collaboration. Contributions are welcome from builders, researchers, and organizations working on AI systems, data, and governance.

---

## Guiding Principles

Before contributing, please align with the core intent of the framework:

- Open and voluntary
- Non-prescriptive (no forced standards)
- Focused on structure, measurement, and stability
- Designed to complement existing systems, not replace them

---

## What You Can Contribute

We welcome contributions in the following areas:

### 1. Knowledge Block Examples
- New block definitions (industry-specific or general)
- Improvements to existing examples
- Better structuring of context, relationships, or logic

### 2. Schema Improvements
- Enhancements to JSON schemas
- Validation rules and refinements
- Additional fields that improve clarity or usability

### 3. Documentation
- Clarifications or simplifications
- Real-world use cases
- Diagrams or visual explanations

### 4. Measurement & Evaluation
- Information Yield (IY) examples
- Scoring approaches
- Benchmarking ideas

### 5. Runtime & Execution
- Runtime layer improvements
- Execution patterns
- Safety and fallback logic

---

## How to Contribute

1. Fork the repository  
2. Create a new branch:
3. Make your changes
4. Submit a Pull Request with:
- Clear title
- Description of what you added/changed
- Why it improves the framework

---

## Contribution Guidelines

- Keep contributions simple and readable
- Avoid over-engineering
- Prefer clarity over complexity
- Use consistent naming conventions
- Document your changes

---

## Review Process

All contributions will be reviewed for:

- Alignment with AIPA principles
- Clarity and usability
- Relevance to real-world AI systems

Feedback may be provided before merging.

---

## Code of Conduct

- Be respectful
- Assume positive intent
- Focus on improving the work, not individuals

---

## License

By contributing, you agree that your contributions will be part of this open framework and governed by the repository’s license.

---

## Final Note

AIPA is evolving.

This is not a fixed standard — it’s a shared effort to make AI systems more understandable, measurable, and reliable.

If you’re thinking deeply about these problems, you’re in the right place.
