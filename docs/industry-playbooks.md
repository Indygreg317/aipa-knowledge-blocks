# Industry Playbooks

Industry Playbooks are curated collections of Knowledge Blocks tailored to specific domains.

They provide structured, reusable decision frameworks aligned with real-world operational, regulatory, and risk environments.

## Purpose

To organize Knowledge Blocks into domain-specific systems that can be:

- deployed in real environments
- aligned with industry standards
- adapted to organizational needs
- extended over time

## What a Playbook Contains

A playbook may include:

- domain-specific Knowledge Blocks
- policy-driven decision logic
- execution control configurations
- governance and approval patterns
- verification and trust requirements
- example scenarios and workflows

## Example Domains

### Compliance
- regulatory requirements
- audit workflows
- approval chains
- policy enforcement

### Operations
- production decision logic
- scheduling and capacity controls
- incident response actions
- escalation procedures

### Carbon / Sustainability
- emissions tracking logic
- reporting validation
- methodology enforcement
- audit and compliance checks

## How Playbooks Work

Playbooks do not replace the core framework.

They apply the framework to a domain.

Each Knowledge Block still follows:

- schema structure
- execution control rules
- governance and verification

The playbook simply defines:

- what decisions matter
- how they are structured
- how they are used together

## Key Principle

A framework defines how decisions work.

A playbook defines which decisions matter in a given domain.

## Outcome

Industry Playbooks enable organizations to move from abstract frameworks to practical, deployable systems.
