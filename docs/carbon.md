# Carbon / Sustainability

Carbon and sustainability use cases apply Knowledge Blocks to emissions tracking, reporting validation, and regulatory compliance.

## Purpose

To ensure that:

- emissions data is accurate and complete
- reporting follows approved methodologies
- submissions are validated at execution time
- compliance is auditable

## How Knowledge Blocks Are Used

Knowledge Blocks define:

- reporting requirements
- calculation methods
- validation rules
- approval workflows

## Execution Control in Carbon Systems

At runtime, execution control ensures:

- reporting periods are valid
- methodologies are current and approved
- required data is present
- risk and compliance thresholds are met

If conditions fail:

- submissions are rejected or deferred
- escalation is triggered
- audit records are created

## Key Principle

Sustainability decisions must be validated under current conditions, not assumed from prior approval.

## Audit and Traceability

Each action includes:

- Knowledge Block ID
- validation outcome
- supporting evidence
- timestamp

## Outcome

Carbon and sustainability processes become:

- consistent
- verifiable
- enforceable
- auditable
