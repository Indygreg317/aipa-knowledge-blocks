# Compliance

Compliance defines how Knowledge Blocks are used to represent, enforce, and audit regulatory and policy-driven decisions.

## Purpose

To ensure that:

- policies are encoded as structured decisions
- enforcement happens at execution time
- compliance is auditable and traceable
- violations are prevented, not just detected

## How Compliance Uses Knowledge Blocks

Knowledge Blocks represent:

- regulatory requirements
- internal policies
- approval workflows
- control conditions

Each block includes:

- decision logic
- constraints
- execution control rules
- verification requirements

## Execution Control in Compliance

At runtime, execution control ensures that:

- policies are still valid
- regulatory conditions are met
- authority is enforced
- risk remains within acceptable limits

If conditions fail:

- execution is blocked
- escalation paths are triggered
- audit records are created

## Key Principle

Compliance is not just documentation.

It is enforced at the moment of execution.

## Audit and Traceability

All compliance-related decisions include:

- Knowledge Block ID
- execution outcome
- reason for decision
- timestamp
- reviewer or system actor

This creates a complete, auditable record.

## Outcome

Compliance becomes:

- enforceable
- consistent
- transparent
- auditable

rather than static and retrospective.
